Usual Resident Population by Sex and Age for Wirral

A text component
 

Introduction

Understanding and planning for population change is one of the key factors in service planning for local authorities. This report is one of a series of reports in LG Inform based on a single collection; the aim is to provide users with quick and easy access to all the metrics contained in the collection and present this in a variety of charts, tables and maps.

This report provides an overview of the "Census 2021 rounded population and household estimates for local authorities in England and Wales, by sex and five-year age" as published by the Office for National Statistics, with a particular focus on Wirral. 

Figures relate to the usual resident population or household.

  • A usual resident is anyone who on Census Day, 21 March 2021 was in the UK and had stayed or intended to stay in the UK for a period of 12 months or more, or had a permanent UK address and was outside the UK and intended to be outside the UK for less than 12 months.
  • A household is:
    • one person living alone: or
    • a group of people (not necessarily related) living at the same address who share cooking facilities and share a living room or sitting room or dining area
    • A household must have at least one usual resident at the address. A group of short-term residents living together or a group of visitors staying at an address is not classified as a household.

Executive summary

Wirral is a Metropolitan District in the North West region. 

On Census Day, 21 March 2021, the size of the usual resident population in Wirral was 320,200 people: this is an increase of 0% (417) since 2011, when it was 319,783 people. Wirral is now ranked 4 (out of 15 Metropolitan Districts in the North West region) in terms of total population. 

Wirral's population increase, at 0%,  compares to a 5% increase for the North West and a 7% increase for England.

As of 2021, Wirral is ranked 9 out of the 41 local authority areas in the North West for population density, with around 19.90 persons per hectare of land. The population density for the North West is 5.26 persons per hectare and for England it is 4.34 persons per hectare

Of the 320,200 people in Wirral, 165,100 were women (51.6% of the population) and 155,100 men (48.4%). 17.0 percent (54,300) are children aged under 15, 61.1 percent (195,600) are adults aged 15 to 64 and 22.0 percent (70,400 ) are aged 65 and over; 2.9 percent (9,300) of the resident population are 85 and over.

Population by sex

Of the 320,200 people in Wirral, 165,100 were women (51.6% of the population) and 155,100 men (48.4%). In 2011, 165,936 were women (51.9% of the population) and 153,847 men (48.1%).

In North West, 50.9% of the population were women and 49.1% were men. In England, 51.0% of the population were women and 49.0% were men.

The female population of Wirral has decreased by -1% and the male population has increased by 1% from 2011. 

In the North West the female population has increased by 5% and the male population has increased by 5% from 2011.

Population by broad age group

Of the 320,200 people living in Wirral on Census day, 17.0 percent (54,300) were children aged under 15, 61.1 percent (195,600) were adults aged 15 to 64 and 22.0 percent (70,400 ) were aged 65 and over; 2.9 percent (9,300) of the resident population were 85 and over.

This compares to 17.6 percent aged 0 to 14, 63.7 percent aged 15 to 64 and 18.7 percent aged 65 and over for the North West region as a whole, and 17.4 percent aged 0 to 14, 64.2 percent aged 15 to 64 and 18.4 percent aged 65 and over for England. 

Since 2011, Wirral  has seen a -2 percent decrease in children aged under 15, a -4 percent decrease in adults aged 15 to 64 and a 15 percent increase in those aged 65 and over.
